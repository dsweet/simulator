# PPE/PPEL Persona Design Brief

## Overview

Eight fictional graduates from the PPE/PPEL track — four from the University of Richmond's PPEL (Philosophy, Politics, Economics & Law) program and four from the University of Rochester's PPE (Politics, Philosophy & Economics) program. Each represents a distinct path these interdisciplinary degrees can become. Four pursue graduate school (JD, MPP, or PhD); four enter careers directly. Course plans use each school's actual curriculum and course IDs.

At Richmond (PPEL), IB/AP credit is very restrictive (7-unit cap, no SL accepted, HL min 5). The student has IB Japanese SL knowledge but no Richmond credit for it — however, proficiency from IB study places them into Intermediate Japanese (JAPN 201/202, the 3rd and 4th courses in the series), satisfying the foreign language requirement. At Rochester (PPE), the open curriculum means only a writing course and two 3-course clusters outside the major are required beyond the PPE major itself.

## Constraints

- 4 of 8 pursue graduate school — 1 JD, 1 MPP, 2 PhD
- 4 have non-academic outcomes with genuine impact
- All driven by ideas and questions, not credentialing
- Every course comes from the existing Richmond PPEL or Rochester PPE course catalog
- Prerequisite chains are respected
- Richmond personas use JAPN 201 and JAPN 202 (3rd and 4th in the Japanese series) for foreign language
- Rochester personas include two 3-course clusters outside the major

## Richmond PPEL Personas (4)

### 1. The Constitutional Advocate — Amara Osei-Mensah
**Brief:** Daughter of Ghanaian immigrants in Northern Virginia, grew up hearing her parents' stories of constitutional crises in Ghana and watching American constitutional debates play out on the news — struck by how similar the underlying questions were. Builds the deepest legal foundation: constitutional law, civil rights & liberties, philosophy of law, and political theory. Goes to Georgetown Law, summers at the ACLU and a constitutional litigation clinic. Becomes a civil rights attorney specializing in voting rights and algorithmic fairness in government systems. Grad school outcome (JD).

### 2. The Policy Analyst — Kai Tanaka-Morrison
**Brief:** Grew up in Portland, OR, where a parent who worked for the city planning office brought home stories of how economic data shaped policy decisions — and how often the data was misread or misused. Takes the economics concentration within PPEL, adding data analysis, computing, and public policy courses. Goes to Princeton's School of Public and International Affairs (SPIA). Becomes a policy analyst specializing in evidence-based program evaluation, eventually leading a state government's data analytics office. Grad school outcome (MPP).

### 3. The Civic Entrepreneur — Sade Abiodun-Williams
**Brief:** Grew up in Atlanta watching her neighborhood change as longtime residents were displaced by development that claimed to be "for the community." Combines PPEL with Richmond's Jepson School of Leadership Studies — one of the only undergraduate leadership programs in the country. After graduation, joins a community land trust, then co-founds a social enterprise that helps neighborhoods create cooperative ownership structures for commercial property. Non-PhD.

### 4. The Bridge Builder — Ethan Kovac-Nguyen
**Brief:** Child of a Croatian refugee father and a Vietnamese-American mother in Richmond, VA, grew up navigating between cultures and languages. Drawn to PPEL's international dimensions: takes international relations, comparative politics, and East Asian history alongside the PPEL core. After graduation, works for an international education nonprofit, then a cultural diplomacy program, eventually running a cross-cultural exchange organization connecting American and East Asian university students in policy dialogue. Non-PhD.

## Rochester PPE Personas (4)

### 5. The Democratic Theorist — Zara Petrov-Williams
**Brief:** Grew up in a dual-citizen household (US/Russia) in upstate New York, watching democratic norms strain in both countries simultaneously. Fascinated by why democratic institutions fail. Takes the deepest philosophy concentration within PPE: formal logic, political philosophy, philosophy of language, metaphysics, and advanced ethics. History cluster traces the arc of democratic thought. PhD at Yale in political philosophy, studying deliberative democracy and how institutional design can make democratic participation meaningful rather than performative. Grad school outcome (PhD).

### 6. The Political Economist — Deshawn Marcus-Okafor
**Brief:** Grew up in Rochester, NY, watching a Rust Belt city try to reinvent itself — some neighborhoods reviving, others left behind, and the politics of who got what was never just about economics. Takes the economics concentration within PPE with extra game theory and international economics. Studio art cluster (ceramics). PhD at Princeton in political economy, studying how cities allocate resources across neighborhoods and why some communities get investment while others get neglect. Grad school outcome (PhD).

### 7. The Civic Technologist — Miriam Feldstein-Choi
**Brief:** Grew up in a politically active Korean-Jewish household in suburban DC, watched her parents organize voter registration drives using increasingly sophisticated digital tools — and noticed that the tools were often designed by people who didn't understand the communities using them. Takes PPE with a digital media cluster and computing courses. After graduation, joins a civic tech nonprofit building tools for participatory budgeting and public engagement. Eventually becomes design director at a national civic technology organization. Non-PhD.

### 8. The Systems Thinker — Owen Ramirez-Nakamura
**Brief:** Son of a Mexican-American environmental engineer and a Japanese-American city planner, grew up in the Bay Area watching his parents tackle connected problems from separate angles — his mom designing water treatment systems while his dad zoned housing that needed the water. Takes PPE with a brain & cognitive science cluster (judgment and decision-making) and environmental studies courses. After graduation, joins a climate policy think tank, then works for a state environmental agency, eventually becoming a policy advisor who bridges the gap between environmental science, economic modeling, and political feasibility. Non-PhD.

## How They Differ

|-----------|-----------|
| **School** | Richmond PPEL (4) · Rochester PPE (4) |

| **PPEL concentration** | Law/Philosophy (Amara) · Economics (Kai) · Leadership (Sade) · Politics/International (Ethan) |
| **PPE concentration** | Philosophy depth (Zara) · Economics depth (Deshawn) · Political Science depth (Miriam) · Economics depth (Owen) |
| **Rochester clusters** | History + Astronomy (Zara) · Studio Art + History (Deshawn) · Digital Media + History (Miriam) · Brain & Cog Sci + Astronomy (Owen) |

| **Career direction** | Civil rights law · Government data analytics · Community land trusts · Cultural diplomacy · Academic political theory · Academic political economy · Civic technology · Climate policy |
